Routes

Layers

Vertiports are land-first: the candidates are real open parcels from OSM (sports pitches, recreation grounds, parks, open grass). We drop any inside the FRZ, keep those within 2.5 km of the route that also have demand within 900 m, and rank by demand — so each pad sits on a surface you can actually land on. Villages swallowed by the FRZ cannot host one. The route is computed, not drawn: a tangent-to-circle avoidance bows high and wide around the FRZ (~3.9 km stand-off from the edge) on the east side. The Waddington protected area is shown as a simplified EGRU319 WADDINGTON Flight Restriction Zone shape (circle with a squared stub at each end), cross-checked visually against Drone Safety Map / Altitude Angel. Lengths and closest-approach distances are real. Endpoints/RAF coords are real public places; verify against the UK AIP/NATS AIS before any real operation.

Method (read me)

1. Map real demand from OpenStreetMap (hospitals, schools, restaurants, parks, civic, transport, retail).
2. Overlay regulated airspace — RAF Waddington 5 km FRZ + MATZ, HMP Lincoln 400 m prison FRZ.
3. Score a 120 m grid by weighted demand within 650 m, minus exclusions, +open-space bonus.
4. Keep adding spaced sites until 85% demand coverage → the count is derived, not fixed.
